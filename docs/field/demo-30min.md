# Lumio 30-Minute Buyer Deep-Dive

Date: 2026-03-11
Audience: buyer committee, admin stakeholders, governance stakeholders
Goal: show both buyer narrative and operational credibility

## Opening framing

Start with these points:

- Lumio is a governed enterprise workspace preview.
- It is an enterprise workspace platform, not a consumer workflow app.
- The product backbone is the OA v1 nine-role model.
- Okta OIDC is the only enterprise login target in scope.
- `current workspace` is the more credible enterprise path being deepened.
- `local_lab` is a sandbox / preview workspace used for guided evaluation.
- We are not claiming full production closure or full pilot closure.

## Agenda

1. Product positioning
2. Workspace shell and role model
3. Governed flow on one task
4. Members & Access
5. Policy & Governance Center
6. Integration & Readiness Center
7. Audit & Reporting Center
8. Q&A on maturity and deployment boundary

## 1. Product positioning

Say:

- Lumio is designed as one enterprise workspace shell.
- The goal is not to add another isolated admin console.
- The value is the connection between governed flow and control plane.

## 2. Workspace shell and role model

Show:

- Workspace Overview
- OA role switcher
- workspace page vs role page

Say:

- The OA v1 nine-role model is not decorative.
- It is the operating backbone for how the governed flow and control plane are explained.

## 3. Governed flow on one task

Walk one task through:

1. Request Center
2. Approval Center
3. Operations Console
4. Review Center
5. Audit & Reporting Center

Call out each time:

- current stage
- next action
- blocked reason
- who is waiting
- audit receipt
- evidence bundle

Key point:

- same task
- same evidence bundle
- different role-aware views

## 4. Members & Access

Show:

- seat scope
- boundary history
- change history
- invite lifecycle
- join trace
- workspace admin boundary visibility

Say:

- This is still not a standalone IAM product.
- It is the workspace participation layer needed to explain who can carry which part of the governed flow.

## 5. Policy & Governance Center

Show:

- policy basis
- decision trace
- decision rationale
- exception / waiver state
- blocked reason linkage back to policy basis

Say:

- Policy is credible here because it explains the live blocker, not because it claims a broad policy platform beyond the implemented shell.

## 6. Integration & Readiness Center

Show:

- readiness checklist
- gate owner
- gate state
- why ready / why not ready
- next action
- gate transitions

Say:

- provider scope stays intentionally narrow
- Okta OIDC is the only target in scope
- this keeps the readiness framing honest and aligned to what exists

## 7. Audit & Reporting Center

Show:

- audit receipt
- receipt completeness
- traceability view
- evidence bundle continuity
- filter clarity
- export boundary

Say:

- audit receipt and evidence bundle are visible today
- export remains preview-only framing, not a claim of full production-grade audit export operations

## 8. Close

Summarize in this order:

- what is already coherent
- what is already buyer-credible
- what is intentionally preview
- what is deferred without weakening the current product value

## Mature but honest close

Use wording like:

- “The product is already credible as a governed enterprise workspace preview.”
- “The current workspace path is becoming stronger, but we are not claiming full pilot or production closure.”
- “The value today is the governed flow plus the role-aware control plane, not unsupported provider breadth or fake rollout claims.”
