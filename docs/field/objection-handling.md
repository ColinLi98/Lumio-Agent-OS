# Lumio Objection Handling

Date: 2026-03-11
Audience: buyer-facing field team, product lead, demo host

## Working rule

Answer objections with implemented facts, not future promises.

## 1. “Isn’t this just a demo?”

Short answer:

No. It is a governed enterprise workspace preview with implemented flow, control-plane sections, explicit guardrails, and current-workspace reliability coverage.

Expanded answer:

- It is still a preview, so we do not overclaim production closure.
- But it is more than a static demo shell:
  - governed flow is implemented
  - OA v1 nine-role model is implemented
  - current-workspace path has route, CTA, and smoke guardrails
  - control-plane sections share the same blocker, readiness, and audit context

Show in product:

- Workspace Overview
- current-workspace role/task continuity
- Policy / Governance
- Audit & Reporting

## 2. “Why only Okta OIDC?”

Short answer:

Because Okta OIDC is the only supported login target in scope today, and we keep that boundary explicit rather than implying coverage we do not have.

Expanded answer:

- Narrow provider scope is intentional in this phase.
- The value being sold now is the governed enterprise workspace platform, not broad IdP breadth.
- Readiness and diagnostics are stronger when they stay tied to one real target.

## 3. “Why is current workspace not full production closure yet?”

Short answer:

Because the product is a governed enterprise workspace preview, not a claim of full production rollout completion.

Expanded answer:

- Current workspace is becoming more credible and now carries stronger reliability and control-plane guardrails.
- We still keep unsupported or ambiguous enterprise writes fail-closed.
- That is the honest boundary for this phase.

## 4. “If you already have the shell, aren’t you basically done?”

Short answer:

The shell matters, but the real value is the governed flow plus role-aware control plane plus honest guardrails.

Expanded answer:

- What is already strong:
  - governed flow
  - OA role model
  - control-plane sections
  - current-workspace reliability baseline
- What is still intentionally partial:
  - broader provider coverage
  - full production closure
  - full pilot closure
  - unsupported live mutation breadth

## 5. “Why don’t you have more real deployment proof yet?”

Short answer:

We do not use unsupported claims to substitute for proof. Instead we use implemented product facts and proof-pack artifacts.

Expanded answer:

- We can show:
  - governed flow continuity
  - current-workspace mutation guardrails
  - readiness framing
  - audit receipt and evidence bundle continuity
- We do not claim:
  - full pilot closure
  - full production rollout closure

## 6. “Is the governance layer just UI packaging?”

Short answer:

No. The governance value comes from how the system links blocker, policy basis, readiness, member boundaries, audit receipt, and evidence bundle inside one workspace.

Expanded answer:

- blocked reason links back to policy basis
- readiness explains why work can or cannot progress
- audit receipt and evidence bundle carry the same task context
- mutation boundaries are explicit, not hidden

Show in product:

- Policy & Governance Center
- Integration & Readiness Center
- Audit & Reporting Center

## Field rule

Never answer an objection by widening product scope beyond:

- enterprise workspace platform
- governed flow
- OA v1 nine-role model
- Okta OIDC-only target
- current-workspace depth in progress
- `local_lab` sandbox / preview
